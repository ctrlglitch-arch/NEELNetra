02  /  Pollutants

What Contaminates
Our Waters

🫧
Organic Waste
Sewage and biological discharge deplete dissolved oxygen, triggering dead zones where aquatic life cannot survive. The BOD spike signals acute contamination.
↑ BOD  ·  ↓ Dissolved O₂  ·  Eutrophication
⚗️
Industrial Effluent
Heavy metals — lead, arsenic, cadmium — discharged from tanneries, textile mills, and chemical factories bind to sediment and bioaccumulate through the food chain.
Pb  ·  As  ·  Cd  ·  Cr  ·  Hg
🧴
Microplastics
Fragments under 5mm infiltrate aquatic food webs and human tissue. Single-use plastics photodegrade into near-invisible particles, resistant to conventional filtration.
< 5mm  ·  Persistent  ·  Endocrine-disrupting
03  /  Safety

Protecting
Human Health

01
Avoid Direct Contact
Polluted river water carries pathogens causing typhoid, cholera, and hepatitis A. Avoid wading, swimming, or skin exposure during elevated contamination alerts.
02
Boil Before Use
Bringing water to a rolling boil for at least one minute destroys bacteria, viruses, and protozoa. Allow to cool in a covered container before consumption.
03
Multi-Stage Filtration
Combine sediment pre-filters, activated carbon, and UV sterilisation for comprehensive protection against chemical and biological contaminants.
04
Monitor Children
Children are more susceptible to waterborne toxins due to lower body weight and developing immune systems. Enforce strict hygiene around any river activity.
05
Report Incidents
Visible pollution events — foam, discoloration, fish kills — should be reported to the Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) or State PCBs immediately.
06
Source Alternatives
During high-risk periods, switch to verified municipal supply, certified bottled water, or community RO systems. Avoid irrigation with untreated river water on edible crops.
